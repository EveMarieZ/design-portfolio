Eve Zaunbrecher: Web Designer & Design Historian

Hi! My name is Eve. Iâ€™m a web designer, developer, & design historian.

I studied History of Design at the Royal College of Art in London, and wrote my MA dissertation on eighteenth-century French harps. They are beautiful and outrageous. After my journey into this world of rococo nonsense, I learned to code with Skillcrush and am currently scheming with other harp nerds to create a comprehensive harp research repository. Most recently, I worked as a web designer for Stuller, the largest fine jewelry manufacturer in North America. I just really love sparkly gold things!

Find my work on BeHance or get out your fanciest quill pen and write to me.

What I can do

Web Design

Design is first because it is my favorite. From initial concept through wireframes, mockups, and final designs, I love to create elegant and intuitive designs for websites and digital marketing.

Front-End Development

I code websites by hand with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and jQuery. I can also create custom Wordpress sites with basic PHP. Right now I work with frameworks like Bootstrap, Vue.js, and Nuxt.js.

Historical Research

I have a BA in History and MA in History of Design with a special emphasis on research and writing. I can perform research for individuals and institutions, and have experience writing for museum exhibitions and history blogs.

Harp Talks

If you are lucky enough to have an early pedal harp (1700-1800), I would love to tell you more about it! I do lectures and consultations about the early pedal harp and have spoken at the Victoria and Albert Museum, Philadelphia Museum of Art, and the Deustches Museum, Munich.
